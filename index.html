About

Since January 2018, I’ve been deeply engaged in teaching and mentoring, beginning as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant and Student Mentor before joining as a full-time Lecturer in September 2019. Over the years, I’ve taught thousands of learners across CSE110 (Structured Programming) and CSE111 (Object-Oriented Programming). I’m known for a calm, student-first style—listening carefully, explaining concepts repeatedly when needed, and keeping materials up-to-date. I’ve served as Course Coordinator for both CSE110 and CSE111, and as Undergraduate Co-Coordinator, handling offerings, schedules, assessments, and student support across the program.

I also build systems and processes: redesigned lab sheets, migrated course content between languages (Python ↔ Java), structured the entire Student Tutor management system including automated hour/payroll tracking, supported the university’s buX platform, and helped test and refine the Student Management System (Connect) via structured UAT. As Student Tutor Coordinator, I’ve managed large teams of tutors semester after semester, designing routine-aware schedules, coaching, and maintaining a structured feedback loop to strengthen both student learning and tutor development.

On research, I supervise thesis groups and publish in machine learning and applied computing. Beyond academia, I’m a tech enthusiast who actively explores the world of CPUs and GPUs—following new architectures, performance trends, and their role in shaping modern computing. I also share my teaching and learning journey through my YouTube channel Learn with Tawhid.
